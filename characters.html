Character Biographies

Kuzco

Emperor

Voice Actor: David Spade

Kuzco is a self-centered, sarcastic teenage emperor who learns a big lesson in humility after being turned into a llama. Through his wild journey to return to human form, he discovers the value of friendship, kindness, and not making people homeless for a summer getaway.

Pacha

Humble Villager

Voice Actor: John Goodman

Pacha is a kind-hearted peasant who lives in a quiet village int he mountains. He reluctantly reams up with Kuzco to help him get back to the palace, and through their adventure, he becomes the emperor's first true friend and moral compass.

Yzma

Formal Royal Advisor

Voice Actor: Eartha Kitt

Yzma is Kuzco's power-hungry ex-advisor, known for her dramatic flair and wild plans. After being fired, she hatches a ridiculous scheme to take over the empire - with lots of potions, traps, and cackling along the way.

Kronk

Henchman with a Heart of Gold

Voice Actor: Patrick Warburton

Kronk is Yzma's loyal (but very clueless) sidekick. Despite working for the villain, he's sweet, loves cooking, and has a strong moral compass - sometimes guided by conversations between his shoulder angel and devil.

Chicha

Pacha's Wife

Voice Actor: Wendie Malick

Chicha is smart, no-nonsenese, and fiercely supportive of her family. She's pregnant, but don't underestimate her - she handles chaos like a pro and helps keep things running smoothly while Pacha is away.

Tipo & Chaca

Pacha and Chicha's Kids

Voice Actor: Eli Russell (Tipo), Kellyann Kelso (Chaca)

Tipo is the energetic son and Chaca is the sassy daughter. These two bring humor and heart to the film, and they're quick to join in on a good prank to helpr their parents.

Bucky the Squirrel

Squeaky Critter

Voice Actor: Bob Bergen

Bucky may be small, but he plays a big role in Kuzco's journey. Known for his high-pitched squeeks and a grudge against Kuzco, Bucky is a surprisingly influential forest critter.

Theme Song Guy

Musical Narrator

Voice Actor: Tom Jones

He sings the catch "Kuzco... Kuzcooo!" opening song and sets the tone for the movie. He's not in the movie for long, but his intro is unforgettable.

The Old Man (Thrown Out the Window)

Unlucky Palace Visitor

Voice Actor: John Fiedler

He speaks up against Kuzko's arrogance - and gets tossed out a window for it. He pops up a couple of times in the movie with perfect comedic timing

Waitress

Diner Waitress

Voice Actor: Patti Deutsch (Waitress), Jeff Bennett (Chef)

The waitress works at the jungle diner where chaos ensues as Kuzco, Pacha, Yzma, and Kronk all show up. Her reaction to the madness is one of the funniest scenes in the film.
